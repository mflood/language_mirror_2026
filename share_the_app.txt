



  1. Set version/build number — In the project target's General tab, bump the version and build number
  2. Select device — Choose "Any iOS Device (arm64)" as the destination (not a simulator)
  3. Archive — Product → Archive (builds a release archive)
  4. Distribute — When the Organizer window opens, select the archive → "Distribute App" → "App Store Connect" → follow the prompts
  5. TestFlight / Submit — In https://appstoreconnect.apple.com, the build appears under your app's TestFlight tab. From there you can
  test or submit for review.


  # 1. Archive
  cd LanguageMirror
  xcodebuild archive \
    -scheme LanguageMirror \
    -archivePath ./build/LanguageMirror.xcarchive \
    -destination 'generic/platform=iOS'

  # 2. Export for App Store upload
  xcodebuild -exportArchive \
    -archivePath ./build/LanguageMirror.xcarchive \
    -exportPath ./build/export \
    -exportOptionsPlist ExportOptions.plist

  # 3. Upload to App Store Connect
  xcrun altool --upload-app \
    -f ./build/export/LanguageMirror.ipa \
    -u your@apple.id \
    -p @keychain:AC_PASSWORD


  1. Archive & upload to App Store Connect (the steps I just described)
  2. In https://appstoreconnect.apple.com, go to your app → TestFlight tab
  3. Add her Apple ID email as an external tester (or create a group)
  4. Apple does a quick beta review (usually <24 hours for the first build)
  5. She gets an email invite → installs the TestFlight app from the App Store → taps the link → done
